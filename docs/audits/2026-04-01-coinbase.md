# Sloppypasta Audit: Coinbase

**Date**: 2026-04-01
**URL**: https://coinbase.com
**Type**: Web app + mobile apps (iOS, Android)
**Audit Mode**: External (V1, single-agent)
**Conditions**: `[if:content]`, `[if:identity]`, `[if:payments]`, `[if:mobile]`
**Applicable Checks**: 45 of 46 (1 N/A: 6.12 relay/node independence)

---

## Overall: 40% -- Grade D

Coinbase is the largest US-regulated cryptocurrency exchange, publicly traded (COIN), serving 98+ million users with a polished financial application. The product earns its points in design and craft -- the custom design system with proprietary typeface, WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility commitment, consistent UI across trading, staking, and learning sections, and active engineering blog with developer changelogs all reflect a well-resourced team that cares about the surface experience. Where Coinbase collapses under the Sloppypasta framework is sovereignty and privacy -- the category weighted at 20% and the one that defines the difference between a financial product that works for you and one that works on you. Government ID is mandatory for any functionality beyond price browsing, making identity sovereignty a permanent 5%. The exchange is fully custodial (your keys, their safe), payment privacy is structurally impossible because KYC precedes all transactions, and the platform shares user data with Meta and AppLovin for advertising -- constituting double-dipping on a product that already charges trading fees, spread markups, and staking commissions. The spread markup (approximately 0.50%, reaching 2% in volatile conditions) is not itemized at checkout, and the Coinbase One subscription marketed as "zero trading fees" still silently applies the spread. A 2025 insider breach exposed 70,000 users' KYC data, demonstrating the real cost of mandatory identity collection. Coinbase is a competent product built on a model fundamentally hostile to user sovereignty.

## Priority Improvements

### 1. Remove Advertising Tracker SDKs from a Financial Product

**Check**: 5.5b Third-party trackers (current: 20%), 5.5c Analytics philosophy (current: 25%), 5.8 No double-dipping (current: 30%)
**Observed**: Meta Pixel and AppLovin SDK in a financial product that already charges trading fees, spread markups, and staking commissions. User financial behavior data flows to advertising networks for cross-site profile building. The privacy policy acknowledges this may constitute a "sale/share" under CCPA. For a product handling people's money, sending their financial activity data to Meta and AppLovin is a fundamental trust violation.
**Target**: Remove all advertising tracker SDKs. Replace analytics with self-hosted Plausible or Umami. This would raise 5.5b to 75%, 5.5c to 75%, and 5.8 to 50-60%, lifting the 5.5 composite from 25% to approximately 45% and Cat 5 by approximately 5 points.
**Impact**: Beyond score improvement, removing ad trackers from a financial product is a basic fiduciary responsibility. Proton's analysis of the 2025 breach explicitly noted the "true cost of data collection" -- less data held means less data exposed.

### 2. Itemize the Spread as a Separate Fee Line

**Check**: 5.10 No dark patterns (current: 35%), 7.1 Business model visible (current: 45%)
**Observed**: The ~0.50% spread markup (reaching 2% in volatile conditions) is baked into the quoted price and never shown as a separate line item. Coinbase One is marketed as "zero trading fees" while the spread still applies on every simple trade, making the marketing functionally misleading. Users report feeling "misled" with words like "fraudulent" and "exploitative" appearing in fee-related reviews (13% of all reviews, 45% of 1-star ratings).
**Target**: Display "Trading fee: $X + Spread: $Y = Total cost: $Z" on every order confirmation. Update Coinbase One marketing to "Zero trading fee (spread still applies)." This would improve 5.10 to 55-60% and 7.1 to 60%, lifting Cat 5 and Cat 7 by approximately 3-5 points each.
**Impact**: Transparent fee display is the single most impactful trust-building change Coinbase could make with zero engineering complexity.

### 3. Offer a Non-Custodial Exchange Mode

**Check**: 6.11 Key management (current: 5%), 6.13 Custodial vs non-custodial (current: 25%)
**Observed**: The exchange is fully custodial -- Coinbase holds all private keys, can freeze accounts at will, and the 2025 insider breach demonstrated the concrete risk of centralized custody (70K users exposed). The separate Coinbase Wallet app is non-custodial, but the exchange product that handles the majority of user volume is a traditional custodial model.
**Target**: Integrate Coinbase Wallet-style self-custody as an option within the exchange flow -- users could choose custodial (convenience) or self-custody (sovereignty). Even offering key export would move 6.11 from 5% to 50%. Combined with improved custody options, Cat 6 could lift from 21% to approximately 30%.
**Impact**: Self-custody options would differentiate Coinbase from traditional finance while reducing the custodial attack surface that the 2025 breach exploited.

### 4. Reduce Identity Requirements for Small Transactions

**Check**: 6.3 Identity sovereignty (current: 5%), 5.1 No access gates (current: 30%)
**Observed**: Government ID is required for any functionality -- even viewing your own portfolio after initial setup. The product chose to operate in a fully KYC-regulated domain, which permanently anchors identity sovereignty at the 5% floor. This is the single largest drag on the overall score, as the 5% flows through the geometric mean of Cat 6 (weighted at 20%).
**Target**: While full KYC elimination isn't realistic for a regulated US exchange, tiered verification (email-only for small amounts, ID for larger amounts) could raise 6.3 from 5% to 35-50%, which would lift Cat 6 from 21% to approximately 28-32% and the overall score by approximately 2-3 points.
**Impact**: Even within regulatory constraints, proportional identity requirements (small transactions with lighter verification) would meaningfully improve the sovereignty score.

### 5. Fix Customer Support Quality

**Check**: 7.5 Dialogue, not broadcast (current: 40%), 4.1 Shipped ready (current: 60%)
**Observed**: 1.3 stars on Sitejabber (826 reviews); Trustpilot reviewers describe being "forced into an infuriating automated bot system"; "customer service, bank account and phone number" are the top three complaint categories; for a financial product handling billions in user assets, support quality is a product quality issue. The gap between the polished trading interface and the support experience is the most glaring inconsistency in the product.
**Target**: Invest in human support scaling, reduce bot-first routing for financial issues, publish response time SLAs. Improving support quality would raise 7.5 toward 55-60% and 4.1 toward 70%, lifting Cat 7 and Cat 4 by approximately 3-5 points each.
**Impact**: For a financial product, support quality is not optional -- it's a core product feature.

## Methodology

This audit uses the Sloppypasta Framework V1 -- 46 checks across 8 weighted categories, scored on a 5-100% scale with a 5% floor. Category scores are geometric means of applicable checks. The overall score is a weighted geometric mean of category scores with weights: {1:0.10, 2:0.10, 3:0.10, 4:0.10, 5:0.15, 6:0.20, 7:0.15, 8:0.10}. Geometric means penalize low scores disproportionately -- a single 5% score drags harder than a single 95% helps.

**Note on data gathering**: Coinbase.com returns HTTP 403 to automated fetching tools (WebFetch), preventing direct content analysis of the landing page, Learn articles, privacy policy, and cookie policy.
