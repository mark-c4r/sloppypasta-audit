The internet's original sin was lacking native money. Attention became the surrogate currency, producing surveillance, addiction, censorship, and sloppypasta. The antidote is not a better platform but a return to protocols: Nostr for speech, Bitcoin for value. A product either respects these principles or it participates in the decay.

Axioms

Non-negotiable truths from which all heuristics derive. The worldview behind the test.

A1 Gigi
Information wants to be free
Zero marginal cost of reproduction. Paywalls and DRM fight nature and lose.
A2 Gigi
Protocols have speakers; platforms have users
Free speech platforms are an oxymoron. Only protocols guarantee expression.
A3 Gigi
Cryptography is necessary but not sufficient
Without computational proof, you fall back to trusting authorities.
A4 Gigi
Monetary value is human action distilled
"Talk is cheap. Sats are not." Views can be faked. Payments cannot.
A5 Gigi
Identity is prismatic
Forcing a single legal identity is surveillance. Pseudonymity is a feature.
A6 Gigi Vervaeke
Smart is not wise
LLMs can pattern-match but cannot care. Caring requires embodiment.
A7 Gigi
Freedom by design, not by policy
"Don't be evil" fails. "Can't be evil" works. Exit costs must approach zero.
A8 37signals
Scope is the variable, not time
Build less. Say no by default. Half, not half-assed. Constraint is a blessing.

Heuristic Categories

I. Content Integrity (Anti-Slop) 20%
Does this product resist the flood of low-care, machine-generated content?
  • CI-0
    No access gates on content
    Paywalls, email harvesting modals ("enter email to continue reading"), forced account creation to view content, "sign up to see more" — all antipatterns. Information wants to be free (A1). If the content is good, let people pay voluntarily after reading. Gating access trades sovereignty for conversion metrics.
    Gigi
  • CI-1
    No detectable LLM smell
    Negation structures, em-dash density, false emphasis, sycophantic patterns, "mid" authority voice
    Gigi
  • CI-2
    Human editorial judgment visible
    Evidence of curation, selection, editorial voice — not just aggregation
    37signals
  • CI-3
    Proof of care
    Named author, iteration history, corrections — someone stood behind this
    Shared
  • CI-4
    Information density
    Signal-to-noise ratio. Padded with filler, engagement bait, or related-articles spam?
    Gigi
  • CI-5
    Provenance verifiable
    Content signed, timestamped, attributed? Can you trace it to origin?
    Gigi
  • CI-6
    No drive-by contributions
    If open-source: AI-generated PRs filtered? Maintainer craft protected?
    Gigi
II. Sovereignty & Privacy 20%
Does this product respect user autonomy and resist surveillance?
  • SP-1
    The rugpull test
    Can this product rugpull its users? If yes, it fails sovereignty.
    Gigi
  • SP-2
    Exit cost near zero
    Export data, content, identity, relationships? In what format?
    Gigi
  • SP-3
    No identity collection
    Sliding scale: Keypair-only, no account (ideal) → Minimal account, no email required → Email-required but justified by product need → Email harvesting modals, forced signup walls (fail) → Government ID or biometrics (always 0, no exceptions — "legally required" is not an excuse, it's a reason to change jurisdictions). Does identity collection match what the service actually needs, or does it collect more?
    Gigi
  • SP-4
    No surveillance by default
    Analytics, tracking pixels, third-party scripts? What data is collected?
    Gigi
  • SP-5
    Protocol-based, not platform-locked
    Built on open protocols (RSS, Nostr, ActivityPub) or proprietary APIs?
    Gigi
  • SP-6
    User controls the algorithm
    Can users see, modify, or disable recommendation/ranking logic?
    Gigi
  • SP-7
    Self-hostable or verifiable
    Can you run your own instance, or at minimum audit the code?
    Gigi
  • SP-8
    Pay-per-service, not subscription
    PPQ model: pay for what you use, no long-term contracts, no stored payment methods, no recurring charges requiring permission. Sats in, service out.
    Gigi

VII. Wisdom & Restraint 5%
Does this product know when to stop, when to say no, and when to leave you alone?
  • WR-1
    Deliberate constraints
    Constrained by design choice, not by limitation?
    37signals
  • WR-2
    Architecturally unable to nag
    Sliding scale: Product holds no contact data — can't nag by design (ideal) → Has your email but never uses it for re-engagement → Sends occasional useful updates you opted into → "We miss you" campaigns, guilt-tripping, dark-pattern unsubscribe flows (fail). The test: does communication serve you or their retention metrics?
    Gigi
  • WR-3
    Long-term thinking
    Built for decades or for the next funding round?
    Shared
  • WR-4
    LLM as tool, not master
    If AI is used, does it build understanding or bypass it?

VIII. Value Alignment 5%
Does this product align incentives between creators, users, and itself?
  • VA-1
    Value flows to creators
    Creators capture value, or platform extracts it?
    Gigi
  • VA-2
    No attention extraction
    Helps you finish tasks, or maximizes time-on-app?
    Gigi
  • VA-3
    V4V or pay-per-use
    Sliding scale: V4V or pay-per-use with sats (ideal) → One-time purchase → Subscription that genuinely offers better value than per-use (bus ticket: bulk discount, simpler UX) → Flat-rate subscription regardless of usage where the business model depends on people forgetting to cancel (fail — Netflix, ChatGPT charging $20 when you use $0.50). The test: would the company make the same money if every customer was fully informed and actively choosing each month?
    Gigi
  • VA-4
    Curation rewarded
    Values discovery and curation, not just creation?
    Gigi
