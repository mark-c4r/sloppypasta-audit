# The Sloppypasta Audit

Two things broke the internet: no native money, and no taste. The first gave us surveillance advertising and subscription traps. The second gave us sloppypasta: content generated by machines, signed by no one. This framework scores both dimensions. Structural: is this built on protocols that can't rugpull you, or a platform that will? Craft: did someone give a damn about the words, the design, the experience? Sovereignty without craft is ugly. Craft without sovereignty is a gilded cage.

---

## Axioms

These are the worldview. They're not testable per product — they're what we believe.

1. **Care requires stakes.** LLMs have none. No pain, no mortality, no time they'll never get back. You detect care in the words, the design, the attention to detail. You detect its absence faster.
2. **Every default is a decision.** Most users never change settings. What shipped out of the box — what's tracked, what's opted in, what's shown — is the product. Judge the defaults, not the options menu.
3. **The internet shipped without money or identity.** Every dark pattern, every login wall, every surveillance feed is a workaround for those two missing layers. Without native payment, your attention became the product. Without native identity, platforms became the gatekeepers.
4. **If the architecture allows betrayal, the only question is when.** Policy depends on who's in charge. Architecture doesn't. "Don't be evil" lasted until it didn't. "Can't be evil" is enforced by code.
5. **Without proof, someone has to vouch — and that someone becomes the failure point.** Unsigned content needs a certifier. Platform-issued identity needs an issuer. Unanchored history needs a curator. Every trusted third party is a single point of failure waiting to happen.
6. **If you can't see the business model, you are the product.** Hidden revenue means hidden incentives. Follow the money and you find why the dark patterns exist, why the free tier is crippled, and why the notifications won't stop.
7. **Payments can't be faked the way engagement can.** Views, likes, and followers are manufactured at scale. A sat payment requires actual economic commitment. When you need honest signal, follow the money that moved, not the metrics that moved.

## How It Works

47 checks across 8 categories. Each check scores 0-100% on a sliding scale. Most products land in the 20-80% range. Zeros are rare, reserved for actively hostile behavior.

Category scores are the geometric mean of their checks. The overall score is the weighted geometric mean of category scores. Low scores drag disproportionately through multiplication. You can't compensate for a structural failure with nice fonts.

```
category = (h1 × h2 × ... × hN) ^ (1/N)
overall  = cat1^w1 × cat2^w2 × ... × cat8^w8
```

In spreadsheets:

```
category = EXP(AVERAGE(LN(scores/100))) * 100
overall  = EXP(SUMPRODUCT(weights, LN(cats/100))) * 100
```

Why geometric? Arithmetic averages let you compensate. A 15% in Sovereignty costs only ~17 points. Score well elsewhere and you still pass. The geometric mean doesn't forgive. That 15% drags everything through multiplication. A product scoring 15% Sovereignty and 10% Honesty gets 43% (D), not the 59% (C) arithmetic would give.

| Grade | Score | Character |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| A | 85-100 | Sovereign, crafted, honest. Rare. |
| B | 70-84 | Solid. Gaps fixable with intention. |
| C | 55-69 | Care visible in spots. Structural problems underneath. |
| D | 40-54 | Platform thinking. Attention extraction. Carelessness. |
| F | 0-39 | Surveillance product, sloppypasta factory, or fraud. |

## Categories

### Care — did someone give a damn?

Axiom 1: Care requires stakes. These four categories measure whether a human with something to lose sat with the hard questions — in the text, in the design, in the code, in the shipping discipline.

#### 1. Slop Detection — "Can you smell the AI?"

**Weight: 10%**

LLMs produce statistically average output. Useful for drafts. Not for shipping. This category catches machine-generated text and manufactured social proof. If nobody cared enough to write the words or earn the audience, the rest of the audit is grading a corpse.

| # | Check | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1.1 | LLM smell markers | Banned-word frequency, em-dash density, structural patterns (Bold Prefix lists, symmetrical paragraphs), model-specific openers |
| 1.2 | Social authenticity | Real engagement, real followers, real community. Not bots, dead internet signals, or purchased audiences. |

#### 2. Writing & Thinking — "Does the text show a thinking human?"

**Weight: 10%**

Writing is thinking made visible. Sloppy words mean sloppy thinking, or no thinking at all. Good copy guides, orients, instructs. Bad copy fills space. Every heading, label, and error message is a design decision somebody either made or didn't.

| # | Check | Condition | Description |
|---|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 2.1 | Economy of words | Universal | Banned-word density per 1000 words. Filler detected, counted, penalized. |
| 2.2 | Copy is interface design | Universal | Does copy guide and orient? Or is it decorative filler? |
| 2.3 | No throat-clearing | `[if:content]` | No filler openings, no hedging seesaws, no padding paragraphs. |

#### 3. Design & Interface — "Does the look show care?"

**Weight: 10%**

Design is decisions. Tailwind defaults are the absence of decisions. This category measures whether someone looked at the screen and thought about what goes where, how things move, and whether a human can use it without instructions.

| # | Check | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Information density | Items per viewport. Whitespace is intentional grouping, not uniform padding. |
| 3.2 | Physical obviousness | Usable without instruction. Controls look interactive. Affordances clear. |
| 3.3 | Design system consistency | Consistent tokens, spacing, type scale. Resize to 800px tablet width. If layout breaks, AI generated it. |
| 3.4 | Self-evident UI | No tutorial needed. Undo available. Empty states designed, not dead ends. |
| 3.5 | Modal-free experience | No popup-on-load, newsletter overlays, or login-wall interstitials. |
| 3.6 | Accessibility basics | Contrast, tap targets, screen reader landmarks. Broken focus states = AI code tell. |

#### 4. Product Craft — "Less is more"

**Weight: 10%**

Ship it finished or don't ship it. Core flows should work on first use. Features should connect to something. Code should show review, not debug remnants. Products that iterate publicly and document decisions earn trust here.

| # | Check | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Shipped ready | Core flow works. No placeholder data, no spinner-for-everything, no "Something went wrong." |
| 4.2 | Orphaned features | Every feature accessible, working, connected. No dead settings or "coming soon" placeholders. |
| 4.3 | Show me the code | No `console.log` in production, no inline styles mixed with frameworks, no debug remnants. |
| 4.4 | Iteration visible | Changelogs, release notes, commit history. Hard decisions documented publicly. |

### Structure — can they betray you?

Axioms 2-5: defaults, missing money, architecture, proof. These two categories measure whether the product's structure protects you or exposes you.

#### 5. Product Conduct — "How does it behave?"

**Weight: 15%**

Products have manners. Good ones let you in, let you leave, and respect your time between. Bad ones gate content behind signups, manufacture urgency, harvest attention, and make cancellation a maze. This category separates products built for users from products built on users.

| # | Check | Condition | Description |
|---|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 5.1 | No access gates | Universal | No paywalls, forced signups, or login walls blocking content |
| 5.2 | No manufactured urgency | Universal | No countdown timers, fake scarcity, "only 3 left" |
| 5.3 | No nag/re-engagement | Universal | No notification spam, "we miss you" emails, badge manipulation |
| 5.4 | No attention harvesting | Universal | No infinite scroll, autoplay, streak mechanics. Natural endpoints exist. |
| 5.5 | Privacy & tracking | Universal | Composite of 8 sub-checks: cookies, trackers, analytics, consent, DNT, permissions, telemetry, background data |
| 5.6 | RSS or equivalent | `[if:content]` | Machine-readable content feed available |
| 5.7 | Cancel/leave in one click | Universal | Account deletion, unsubscribe, data removal. One step, not a maze. |
| 5.8 | No double-dipping | Universal | Paid means no ads. Don't charge AND monetize attention. |
| 5.9 | No product coercion | Universal | No "download our app" nags, no "better in Chrome" banners |
| 5.10 | No dark patterns | Universal | No confirmshaming, hidden costs, trick questions, roach motels |

#### 6. Sovereignty & Privacy — "Can they rugpull you?"

**Weight: 20%**

If they can rugpull you, everything else is decoration. Does the product run on protocols you can verify, or platforms that can change terms Tuesday? Can you leave with your data? Do you own your identity, or does someone else hold the keys? Architecture answers these questions. Policy doesn't.

| # | Check | Condition | Description |
|---|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 6.1 | Rugpull resistance | Universal | Can they change terms, raise prices, remove features, shut down? |
| 6.2 | Exit cost zero | Universal | Data export in standard formats. No vendor lock-in. |
| 6.3 | Identity sovereignty | Universal | Keypair (best) to gov ID required (worst) |
| 6.4 | Protocol vs platform | Universal | Open protocol (best) to walled garden (worst) |
| 6.5 | Self-hostable or auditable | Universal | Can you run it yourself? Can you verify what it does? |
| 6.6 | Privacy-preserving payment | Universal | Lightning/ecash (best) to credit card only (worst) |
| 6.7 | Source code availability | Universal | FOSS with maintenance (best) to proprietary paid (worst) |
| 6.8 | Provenance traceable | `[if:content]` | Content signed, timestamped, attributed |
| 6.9 | Distribution independence | `[if:mobile]` | Sideloadable + F-Droid (best) to App Store only (worst) |
| 6.10 | No Play Services dependency | `[if:mobile]` | Works on GrapheneOS/CalyxOS without degradation |
| 6.11 | Key management sovereignty | `[if:identity]` | NIP-07 external signing (best) to custodial (worst) |
| 6.12 | Relay/node independence | `[if:messaging/social]` | Works with any relay (best) to locked relays (worst) |
| 6.13 | Custodial vs non-custodial | `[if:payments]` | Non-custodial (best) to fully custodial (worst) |

### Signal — follow the money

Axioms 6-7: hidden business models and faked engagement. These two categories measure whether the product's incentives are visible and honest.

#### 7. Honesty & Transparency — "Does it tell you the truth?"

**Weight: 15%**

If you can't see the business model, you are it. This category measures whether a product says what it does, how it makes money, and where the AI stops and the human starts. Marketing that matches reality. Free tiers that work. Feedback channels that exist.

| # | Check | Condition | Description |
|---|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 7.1 | Business model visible | Universal | Can you see how they make money? |
| 7.2 | No marketing exaggeration | Universal | Claims match reality. No "revolutionary" vaporware. |
| 7.3 | AI disclosed and labeled | Universal | AI-generated content clearly marked |
| 7.4 | No degraded free experience | Universal | Free tier genuinely useful, not crippled to force upgrade |
| 7.5 | Dialogue, not broadcast | `[if:content]` | Reply mechanism, issue tracker, or public forum exists |

#### 8. Economic Alignment — "Who captures the value?"

**Weight: 10%**

Follow the money. A product funded by ads needs your attention, not your satisfaction. One funded by subscriptions needs your inertia, not your use. One funded by direct payment needs to be worth paying for. Revenue structure shapes incentives. Incentives shape behavior.

| # | Check | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 8.1 | Native money vs ads | Revenue from value exchange (sats, purchases) or attention extraction (ads)? |
| 8.2 | Pay per service | Pricing reflects value delivered. Bus-ticket honest. Inertia billing is not. |
| 8.3 | No coerced upsells | No guilt trips, feature hostage-taking, or artificial limitations to force upgrade |

## Scoring Methodology

### Weights

Weights are set from philosophy and validated by calibration, not optimized to produce desired results.

| Category | Weight | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 1. Slop Detection | 10% | If nobody wrote the words, nothing else matters. |
| 2. Writing & Thinking | 10% | Sloppy words are the first visible symptom. |
| 3. Design & Interface | 10% | Design is decisions. Defaults are their absence. |
| 4. Product Craft | 10% | Ship it finished or don't ship it. |
| 5. Product Conduct | 15% | Behavior when nobody's watching. |
| 6. Sovereignty & Privacy | 20% | If they can rugpull you, everything else is decoration. |
| 7. Honesty & Transparency | 15% | If you can't see the business model, you are it. |
| 8. Economic Alignment | 10% | Follow the money. |

| Dimension | Categories | Combined Weight |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Care | Slop + Writing + Design + Craft | 40% |
| Structure | Conduct + Sovereignty | 35% |
| Signal | Honesty + Economic | 25% |

Care weighs most because it's the first thing you notice. Structure weighs next because it determines your long-term fate. Signal closes the gap because it reveals whose interests the product actually serves.

### The 5% Floor

No check scores below 5%. Geometric means collapse toward zero when any input hits zero. The floor prevents a single hostile check from making an entire category meaningless, while still punishing hard. 5% drags through multiplication.

### Conditional Checks

Checks tagged with conditions activate based on product type. Inapplicable checks score N/A and are excluded from the geometric mean, not scored as zero.

| Tag | Activates when |
|-----|---------------|
| `[if:content]` | Publishes or curates editorial content |
| `[if:mobile]` | Distributed as native app |
| `[if:identity]` | Manages user identity |
| `[if:messaging/social]` | Has communication or community features |
| `[if:payments]` | Handles money |

If conditional exclusions drop a category below 2 active checks, exclude the entire category and redistribute its weight proportionally.

### Composite: Check 5.5

Privacy & Tracking is a geometric mean of 8 sub-checks: cookie count, third-party trackers, analytics philosophy, consent quality, DNT respect, minimal permissions, telemetry opt-in, and background data collection. Mobile sub-checks (permissions, telemetry, background) are N/A for web-only products. Full spec in `docs/check-inventory.md`.

For scoring guidance on every check, see `docs/scale-anchors/`. Detection reference for text analysis: `docs/scoring-reference.md`.

## How to Run the Audit

**As a Claude Code skill:**

```
git clone [repo-url]
cd sloppypasta-audit
/sloppypasta-audit [product-url]
```

The skill scores all applicable checks, computes geometric means, assigns a grade, and generates a scorecard with improvement paths.

**As a manual audit:**

1. Visit the product. Inspect network traffic, read terms, test core flows.
2. Score each applicable check 0-100% against scale anchors in `docs/scale-anchors/`.
3. Compute geometric mean per category, then weighted geometric mean overall.
4. Map to grade. Document scores and evidence.

**As a community tool:**

Run the audit on any public product. Publish the scorecard. The more products scored, the sharper the boundaries become.

## How to Interpret Results

**A (85-100)** means protocol-native, human-written, privacy-respecting, honest about money. These products work for you, not on you. The geometric mean makes A nearly impossible to reach without real strength across every category.

**B (70-84)** means solid with real gaps. Someone cares, the structural foundation is sound, weaknesses are fixable with intention. Boris scored 77%: strong sovereignty and economics, weaker design craft.

**C (55-69)** means care visible in places, structural problems underneath. Good writing but hostile conduct. Decent design but platform lock-in. The geometric mean punishes inconsistency between categories.

**D (40-54)** means platform thinking dominates. Professional polish masking attention extraction and carelessness. Competent design, functional features, still harvesting your data.

**F (0-39)** means surveillance product, sloppypasta factory, or fraud. Twitter/X scored 31%: massive engineering budget, professional design team, still F-grade. Three sovereignty checks at 5% sank it. No surface craft saves a structurally hostile product.

The fastest path to a better grade: fix your worst category. The geometric mean amplifies weakness. One category at 20% drags harder than two at 60%. Sovereignty and Conduct carry 35% combined. Structural problems sink scores fastest.

## Calibration

Calibrated against two products at opposite ends. Boris (read.withboris.com): 77%, B-grade. Sovereignty-aligned indie with strong economic alignment, rough design edges. Twitter/X (x.com): 31%, F-grade. Massive resources, professional team, fails on sovereignty, conduct, and economics.

Boris scored higher on all 8 categories. Zero adjustments to weights, anchors, or boundaries were needed. Every anomaly traced to the framework working as designed: geometric mean amplifying floor-score drag, cross-category penalties catching behavioral hostility even in polished products.

Full results: `docs/calibration/calibration-notes.md`.

## Limitations

Products only. Does not measure companies, founders, or regulatory histories.

External audit mode. V1 scores what an outsider can observe. Infrastructure analysis deferred to V2.

46 checks (HIGH measurability). Full inventory has ~59. MEDIUM/LOW checks wait until scale anchors make them repeatable across auditors.

Two-product calibration. B and F ranges validated. A, C, and D boundaries are structurally sound but lack empirical data.

Single-agent scoring. Multi-agent consensus mode exists but inter-auditor reliability is not yet measured.
